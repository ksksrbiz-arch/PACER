# PACER Partner Profit-Share Agreement (Template)

**Parties.** This Agreement ("**Agreement**") is entered into as of the date last signed below ("**Effective Date**") by and between:

- **1COMMERCE LLC**, an Oregon limited liability company with its principal place of business at Canby, Oregon ("**Company**"), and
- `{{PARTNER_LEGAL_NAME}}`, an individual or entity with a principal address of `{{PARTNER_ADDRESS}}` ("**Partner**").

Each, a "**Party**," and collectively, the "**Parties**."

---

## 1. Nature of the Relationship

1.1 **Independent Contractor.** Partner is engaged as an independent contractor. Partner is **not** an employee, officer, member, manager, agent, joint venturer, or partner (in the legal sense) of Company. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, fiduciary relationship, or any form of equity ownership in Company or any of its affiliates.

1.2 **No Equity; No Beneficial Ownership.** Partner acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) Partner is **not** acquiring any membership interest, equity interest, profits interest, option, warrant, or convertible instrument in Company;
(b) Partner's profit-share percentage under Section 3 is contractual only and does **not** constitute "ownership interest," "substantial control," or any form of "beneficial ownership" within the meaning of the Corporate Transparency Act, 31 U.S.C. § 5336, and its implementing regulations at 31 C.F.R. § 1010.380 ("**CTA**"); and
(c) Partner's maximum profit-share shall at no time exceed **twenty-four and nine-tenths percent (24.9%)** of the revenue pool defined in Section 3, specifically to remain below the 25% threshold used to determine beneficial ownership under the CTA.

1.3 **No New Entity Required.** Partner is not required to form a separate LLC, corporation, or other legal entity to operate under this Agreement. Partner may perform the Services as a sole proprietor and be compensated via IRS Form 1099-NEC.

---

## 2. Services

2.1 **Scope.** Partner shall perform the services described in **Exhibit A** ("**Services**"), which may include, without limitation: sourcing expiring domain leads, qualifying leads against Company scoring criteria, performing outreach to registrants, managing monetization listings (redirect, parking, aftermarket, lease-to-own), or such other services as the Parties mutually agree in writing.

2.2 **Manner of Performance.** Partner controls the method, means, hours, and location of performance. Company controls only the deliverables and quality standards. Partner shall supply Partner's own equipment and tools except as expressly provided in **Exhibit A**.

2.3 **Non-Exclusive.** This engagement is non-exclusive. Partner may provide similar services to other parties provided such work does not (a) use Company Confidential Information, or (b) violate Section 6.

---

## 3. Compensation (Profit Share)

3.1 **Revenue Pool.** For each domain asset introduced, originated, or materially advanced by Partner and attributed to Partner in the PACER system ("**Attributed Domain**"), the "**Net Revenue**" means gross revenue collected by Company from that Attributed Domain less:

(a) registrar, marketplace, and payment-processor fees;
(b) direct acquisition cost (auction or drop-catch fee paid by Company);
(c) refunds, chargebacks, and disputed amounts; and
(d) sales tax and similar pass-through taxes.

3.2 **Profit-Share Percentage.** Company shall pay Partner **`{{REV_SHARE_PCT}}`%** of Net Revenue on each Attributed Domain. In no event shall this percentage exceed **24.9%**. Company reserves the right to lower this percentage for future Attributed Domains on written notice; percentages for already-Attributed Domains are locked for the life of the asset.

3.3 **Attribution.** Attribution is established at the time a domain is added to the PACER pipeline with Partner's `partner_id`. A domain may have at most one attributed Partner. Disputes regarding attribution are resolved by Company in good faith; the Partner waives any claim to attribution not reflected in the PACER database.

3.4 **Payment Cadence.** Company shall compute and pay the profit-share monthly, within **fifteen (15) business days** after month-end, by ACH or other mutually acceptable electronic method. A remittance statement listing each Attributed Domain and its Net Revenue shall accompany each payment.

3.5 **Annual Cap (Optional).** If `{{REVENUE_CAP_USD}}` is specified in **Exhibit A**, total payments to Partner in any calendar year shall not exceed that amount; amounts earned above the cap roll forward to the next calendar year.

3.6 **Taxes.** Partner is responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on amounts received under this Agreement. Company shall issue IRS Form 1099-NEC annually in accordance with applicable law. Partner shall deliver a completed IRS Form W-9 prior to the first payment.

---

## 4. Expenses

Partner bears Partner's own expenses. Company shall not reimburse Partner for any expense unless pre-approved in writing.

---

## 5. Intellectual Property

5.1 **Company IP.** All Company pre-existing materials, software, data, scoring models, pipelines, and proprietary lists remain the exclusive property of Company.

5.2 **Work Product.** All deliverables, analyses, research, data compilations, outreach content, and other materials created by Partner in the performance of the Services are "**Work Product**" and shall be the exclusive property of Company upon creation, with Partner hereby irrevocably assigning to Company all right, title, and interest therein worldwide, including all intellectual property rights.

5.3 **Domains Acquired.** Any domain acquired by Company whether or not introduced by Partner is the sole property of Company. Partner has no ownership interest in any domain, registrant record, or related asset.

---

## 6. Confidentiality

6.1 Partner shall hold in strict confidence all non-public Company information disclosed to Partner, including lead lists, scoring criteria, acquisition pricing, revenue data, customer identities, and the terms of this Agreement. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for **three (3) years**.

6.2 Partner shall not publicly disclose that Partner is affiliated with Company without Company's prior written consent, except to the extent required for tax, legal, or regulatory purposes.

---

## 7. Compliance

7.1 **ICANN / UDRP.** Partner shall not engage in cybersquatting, typosquatting, or any activity prohibited by ICANN's Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy ("**UDRP**") or the Anticybersquatting Consumer Protection Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1125(d). Partner shall not introduce any domain that, to Partner's knowledge, infringes a third-party trademark.

7.2 **CAN-SPAM / TCPA.** All outreach performed by Partner shall comply with the CAN-SPAM Act, 15 U.S.C. §§ 7701 et seq., and the Telephone Consumer Protection Act, 47 U.S.C. § 227, including honoring opt-outs and maintaining accurate sender identification.

7.3 **Sanctions.** Partner represents that Partner is not located in, under the control of, or a national or resident of any country or entity subject to U.S. sanctions, and is not on any U.S. government list of prohibited or restricted parties.

7.4 **CTA Reporting.** Partner acknowledges that so long as Partner's profit-share remains at or below 24.9% and Partner does not exercise "substantial control" over Company, Partner is not required to be reported as a beneficial owner of Company under the CTA.

---

## 8. Term and Termination

8.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated as provided below.

8.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement on **thirty (30) days'** written notice.

8.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately on written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **ten (10) business days** of written notice of the breach.

8.4 **Tail Payments.** Upon termination, Partner shall continue to receive the profit-share under Section 3 on Attributed Domains that were attributed to Partner prior to termination, for the life of the asset, subject to the terms of Section 3.

8.5 **Survival.** Sections 1.2, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, and 11 survive termination.

---

## 9. Representations and Warranties

Each Party represents that it has full legal capacity to enter into this Agreement and that its performance will not violate any other agreement or law. Partner further represents that Partner will perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner.

---

## 10. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF SECTIONS 5 (IP), 6 (CONFIDENTIALITY), OR 7 (COMPLIANCE), NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES. EACH PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE TO PARTNER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

---

## 11. Miscellaneous

11.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Oregon, without regard to conflicts of law.

11.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Clackamas County, Oregon, under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association, except that either Party may seek injunctive relief in court to protect its IP or Confidential Information.

11.3 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement (including Exhibit A) constitutes the entire agreement of the Parties on this subject and supersedes all prior oral or written agreements.

11.4 **Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended in a writing signed by both Parties.

11.5 **Assignment.** Partner may not assign this Agreement without Company's prior written consent. Company may assign to an affiliate or in connection with a sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

11.6 **Notices.** Notices shall be delivered by email to the addresses on the signature block, effective on confirmed delivery.

11.7 **Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remainder continues in effect.
